Organs Of A 5-Year Old With A Rare Genetic Disease Has Been Donated For Research By Family

Photo courtesy of Penny Howard

After making a tough decision, the family of a 5-year old girl who died in January after suffering from a very rare genetic disease, has decided to donate their daughter's organs for scientific research in hopes of finding a cure.

Penny Howard, mother to Harper Howard, told WFAA.com "We had to put all the emotions aside and get her where she needed to be so she could continue to make a difference in this world, and, only after that day, could we sit and mourn the fact that she was no longer with us in her body."

According to a report from WFAA.com, Harper suffered from CDKL5, which is a condition that impacts less than 600 people worldwide. The condition would cause her to regularly experience seizures daily, even up to 24 times per day. The news station stated that Harper is the first person diagnosed with CDKL5 to donate his/her organs for scientific research.

Penny told the station that she is at peace knowing her daughter is no longer in pain.

"To me, her first three years of life were the worst possible time of my life— even over [OTHER?] than what we are going through now," Howard told WFAA.com. "For three years, I had to watch her essentially be electrocuted with no plug to pull."

The Howard family has also started a nonprofit in Harper's honor called Hope 4 Harper to further raise research funds to help scientists find a cure for CDKL5.
